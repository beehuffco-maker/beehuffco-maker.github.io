Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 13, 2026

Overview

This privacy policy describes how applications and integrations developed by Blake Huff ("developer," "I," "me") collect, use, and handle data. These applications are private business tools used to manage content publishing, social media automation, and platform integrations on behalf of accounts I own or administer.

Applications Covered

This policy applies to all developer applications created by Blake Huff that integrate with third-party platforms including Meta (Facebook and Instagram), and any other platforms used for business automation purposes.

Data We Access

These applications may access the following types of data through authorized platform APIs:

  • Facebook Page content, posts, and publishing permissions
  • Instagram Business Account content and publishing capabilities
  • Page insights and engagement metrics
  • Business account configuration and settings

All data access is limited to accounts owned or explicitly authorized by the developer. These applications do not collect, store, or process data from third-party users.

How Data Is Used

Data accessed through these applications is used exclusively for:

  • Publishing and scheduling content to owned social media accounts
  • Automating content distribution workflows for business operations
  • Reading performance data to inform content strategy
  • Managing page settings and business configurations

Data is never sold, shared with third parties, used for advertising, or processed for any purpose beyond the operational functions described above.

Data Storage

These applications operate as private automation tools. Access tokens and API credentials are stored locally on secured devices owned by the developer. No user data is stored in external databases or cloud services beyond what the originating platform retains under its own data policies.

Third-Party Platforms

Data shared with or retrieved from third-party platforms (including Meta) is subject to those platforms' own privacy policies and terms of service. I encourage you to review the relevant policies:

  • Meta Privacy Policy
  • Meta Platform Terms

Data Deletion

Since these applications do not store user data externally, there is no user data to delete beyond what is managed directly within the originating platform. To remove app access to your accounts, revoke permissions directly through the relevant platform's settings (e.g., Facebook Settings > Apps and Websites).
